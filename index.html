What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is more than just a buzzword; it's a revolutionary concept that is reshaping the way we think about data, transactions, and trust. At its core, blockchain is a decentralized ledger that records transactions across a network of computers in a secure and transparent manner. Each transaction is encrypted and linked to the previous one, forming a chain of blocks that are immutable and tamper-proof.

Why Blockchain Matters

Blockchain technology offers a myriad of benefits, including enhanced security, increased transparency, and reduced costs. By eliminating the need for intermediaries and centralized authorities, blockchain empowers individuals to take control of their digital assets and conduct peer-to-peer transactions with confidence.

This a typicall demonstration of a block chain each block is connected to the previous one by their address. Blockchain technology relies on cryptographic hash functions to link blocks together. Each block contains a hash of the previous block's data, along with its own data. If you were to change the data in a previous block, it would alter its hash value. Since each subsequent block's hash is based on the previous block's hash, even a small change in one block would lead to a cascading effect, changing the hash values of all subsequent blocks. This breaks the integrity of the blockchain.
